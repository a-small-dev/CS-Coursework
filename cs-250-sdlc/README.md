# Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC) Coursework

This repository contains coursework and assignments completed for my Software Development Lifecycle class as part of my SNHU Computer Science studies.

## Overview

Throughout this course, I explored the principles and practices of modern software development processes, with a strong focus on **Agile methodologies**, particularly **Scrum**. The course emphasized how teams plan, build, and deliver software in iterative cycles while adapting to change and improving continuously.

## What I Learned

- Fundamentals of the Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC)
- Differences between traditional (Waterfall) and Agile approaches
- Core Scrum concepts, including:
  - Sprints and sprint planning
  - Product backlogs and user stories
  - Daily stand-ups
  - Sprint reviews and retrospectives
- Team roles in Scrum:
  - Product Owner
  - Scrum Master
  - Development Team
- Importance of collaboration, communication, and iterative development
- How Agile improves flexibility and delivery in software projects

## Understanding User Needs and User Stories

One of the most important skills developed in this course was learning how to interpret user needs and translate them into functional software requirements. Breaking down problems into **user stories** helped make requirements clearer, more structured, and focused on the end user’s perspective.

User stories provide a simple but effective way to define what a user wants and why they want it. This approach helps ensure that development stays aligned with real user needs rather than assumptions, and it improves communication between team members by creating a shared understanding of goals and expectations.

## Approach to Program Development and Agile Practices

When developing programs, I learned to approach problems in an iterative and incremental way rather than trying to build everything at once. This includes planning features, breaking them into smaller tasks, and continuously refining the solution as development progresses.

Agile processes I hope to incorporate in future development work include:
- Working in short, focused iterations (sprints)
- Regularly reviewing and improving code through feedback
- Using user stories to guide feature development
- Participating in consistent communication through stand-ups or progress updates
- Adapting quickly to changing requirements

## Being a Good Team Member in Software Development

Being an effective team member in software development means contributing both technically and collaboratively. This includes writing clean and maintainable code, communicating clearly with teammates, and being open to feedback and improvement.

It also means taking responsibility for assigned tasks, supporting others when needed, and actively participating in team discussions and planning. Strong teamwork ensures that projects stay on track and that the final product benefits from multiple perspectives and skill sets.

## Key Takeaways

This course helped me understand how real-world software teams operate beyond just writing code. I gained knowledge in structured teamwork, iterative planning, and adapting to changing requirements. The skills that are essential in modern software development environments.

This repository serves as a record of my learning and practical exercises related to SDLC concepts and Agile/Scrum methodology.
