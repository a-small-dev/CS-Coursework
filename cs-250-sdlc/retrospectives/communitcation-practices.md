# Communication Practices

## Overview
Effective communication is one of the most critical components of success in Agile teams. Agile emphasizes collaboration, adaptability, and continuous feedback, all of which depend on clear communication practices and shared understanding. Throughout this course, I explored how Scrum roles, communication strategies, and project management tools contribute to transparency, efficiency, and successful outcomes.

## Reflection
Effective communication is one of the most critical components of success in Agile teams and any other type of work that involves collaboration. Agile methodologies are focused on collaboration, adaptability, and continuous feedback, all of which depend on clear and consistent communication. Throughout this course, as I learned and reflected on the different Scrum roles, I saw that communication practices, team interactions, and project management tools all play a significant role in creating transparency, improving efficiency, and supporting successful project outcomes.

One of the communication practices that creates transparency in a Scrum Team is the use of structured Scrum events, such as daily stand-ups, sprint planning, sprint reviews, and retrospectives, which provide opportunities for team members to communicate, share progress, identify blockers, and align on goals. However, research shows that simply having these events does not necessarily mean effective understanding among team members. Vitacco and Bosshard (2026) explain that Agile events alone are often insufficient to create true shared understanding, and go on to touch on the importance of establishing a common ground among team members. Without making sure you establish this shared understanding, teams may experience miscommunication, inefficiencies, and rework. Task boards or sprint backlogs further support transparency by making project progress visible to all team members, allowing for better coordination and accountability. A relevant personal example is that I developed a construction progress dashboard for my current project using Python and Matplotlib to visually present project data for civil engineers.

An example of effective communication from the vacation travel assignments was the use of discussion boards and structured team updates during sprint activities. These communication practices allowed team members to clearly communicate their responsibilities, progress, and any challenges they were facing. This reflects findings from Espinosa et al. (2018), who note that Agile transformations increase both formal and informal communication, leading to improved collaboration and team awareness. In my experience, increased communication helps prevent misunderstandings and solve problems before they become bigger headaches. 

In addition to communication practices, Agile project-management tools also play a key role in improving team coordination and efficiency. Tools such as Jira and Azure Boards provide centralized platforms where teams can manage tasks, track progress, and share updates. These tools allow all team members to access the same information at any time. They also help organize work, such as user stories and tasks, which help improve workflow and accountability.

Research supports the idea that structured coordination mechanisms enhance communication within Agile teams. Miranda et al. (2025) found that coordination techniques, such as planning poker, promote more balanced participation among team members by distributing speaking time more evenly. This suggests that when communication is structured and supported by tools or techniques, it leads to more inclusive collaboration and improved team dynamics. Project-management tools also help make sure that all team members can contribute and stay informed. 

Overall, I learned this semester that effective communication in Agile teams is not just about frequent interaction but also about creating shared understanding, maintaining transparency, and using the right tools to support collaboration. Scrum events and communication practices provide a foundation for interaction, but they must be supported by clear communication strategies. Agile project-management tools also strengthen communication by organizing information and making it accessible to all team members.
