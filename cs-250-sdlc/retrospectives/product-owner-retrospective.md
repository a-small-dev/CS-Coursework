# Product Owner Reflection

## Overview
This journal reflects on the role and responsibilities of a Product Owner and how I would apply Scrum events in a real project environment.

## Retrospective
As the Product Owner, I believe engaging with users and stakeholders can be one of the most valuable aspects of the role. By directly communicating with users and stakeholders, you can gain insights that cannot be captured solely through data analytics. For example, understanding the types of filters users value, such as price or vacation preferences in the travel assignment, helps the team prioritize features that truly enhance the user experience. Research supports this approach, noting that Product Owners who actively engage with users can better align development priorities with market demands, ultimately improving project outcomes (Magnussen, Havnelid, & Molleri, 2024). Another key benefit of engaging with users and stakeholders is the increased alignment between the product vision and actual development. 

Reflecting on the user stories assignment, I realized how each user story, framed with a clear description, acceptance criteria, and priority, acts like a bridge between what the users want and what the development team builds. User stories can prevent ambiguity, provide goals, and allow the Scrum team to work iteratively toward delivering value. 

The example interview provided helped by asking users to describe their experiences, challenges, and preferences, and I was able to create realistic and actionable user stories. For instance, one insight from a user interview was the importance of having the ability to refine vacation preferences dynamically. Without these conversations, the team might have focused on other things rather than what truly matters to users. Sverrisdottir, Ingason, & Jonasson also noted this point, saying that Product Owners who collect and act on user feedback can guide development more effectively (2014).

A couple of other methods I can think of, such as surveys and beta testing, are necessary to gather user feedback and data. Surveys can reach a broader user base, and beta testing provides real-world feedback on the product’s performance. Incorporating these methods can strengthen user stories, enabling the team to build features that are both usable and meaningful.

Overall, I have learned that user stories serve as a guiding framework, but ongoing feedback collection and clear prioritization are key to producing a successful product. The role of a Product Owner can also be challenging in the sense that you have to embrace both technical and business perspectives and stay closely connected with users to ensure the product delivers on its intended value.
