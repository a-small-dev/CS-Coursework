# Developer Reflection

## Overview
This journal reflects on the role and responsibilities of a Developer and how I would apply Scrum events in a real project environment.

## Retrospective
As a developer, clear communication with the Agile team is essential for keeping development on track, Morandini, Coleti, Oliveira, and Correa (2021) highlight the importance of daily stand-up meetings in enabling transparency and collaboration. These meetings provide an opportunity to clarify requirements, identify blockers, and ensure all team members are on the same page with sprint goals. I would request that the Product Owner provide detailed acceptance criteria for new/revised/updated features and that the tester share any relevant test cases or known issues. Having this information handy would allow me to adjust my development approach and reduce possible backtracking and redoing the work.

Zaidman, Van-Dijk, Bouskila-Yam, and Gazit (2025) also emphasize that structured communication practices within Scrum meetings such as turn-taking, clarifying questions, and focused updates directly improve understanding and reduce miscommunication among team members. Their study shows that teams following these structured interaction patterns are better able to quickly resolve blockers and maintain alignment with sprint objectives. To ensure I receive the responses I need, I would clearly outline my questions in a concise format and follow up during daily stand-ups or direct messages if I need any further clarification. 

Agile methodology provides me with the flexibility to adapt to any change in requirements that would happen to pop up. For example, sprints allow adjustments to be made continuously throughout the project. By taking full advantage of daily meetings, sprint reviews, and communication with the Product Owner and tester, it allows me to quickly reprioritize tasks and maintain progress toward delivering a working and functional product that meets all of the requirements. 

Example email:

Dear (Product Owner) and (tester),

Looking at the revised development plan for the travel software, I want to make sure that I understand the updated acceptance criteria and any test cases I need to be aware of. To continue development, I need the following information:

1.	From the Product Owner: 

    o	Clarification on acceptance criteria for each story, including required functionality such as search filters, booking, and user notifications. 

    o	Are we listing only wellness/detox, or should all vacation types still be available? 

2.	From the Tester: 

    o	Which test cases should I focus on for the travel booking features?

    o	Have any issues been identified so far?

This information will help me make sure I’m implementing the features correctly, that they meet the acceptance criteria, and that I can address any edge test cases.

Thank you for your support and time,

Anthony Small
